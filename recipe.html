Classic Pasta Carbonara

A traditional Roman pasta dish featuring creamy eggs, crispy pancetta, and sharp Pecorino Romano cheese. This simple yet elegant dish comes together in just minutes for a quick weeknight dinner that tastes like you spent hours in the kitchen.

PREP TIME

15 mins

COOK TIME

20 mins

SERVINGS

4 servings

CALORIES

650 kcal

Ingredients

Adjust servings:

4
400g spaghetti or bucatini
150g pancetta or guanciale, diced
4 large eggs, room temperature
100g Pecorino Romano, freshly grated
50g Parmigiano Reggiano, freshly grated
Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
Sea salt, for pasta water
Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Instructions

1

Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add salt generously (about 1 tablespoon per liter of water). The pasta water should taste like the sea.

2

In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs, grated Pecorino Romano, and Parmigiano Reggiano until well combined. Season with freshly ground black pepper. Set aside.

3

In a large skillet over medium heat, cook the diced pancetta until crispy and golden, about 5-7 minutes. The fat should render out and the meat should be crisp. Remove from heat but keep the rendered fat in the pan.

Chef's Tip

For authentic carbonara, guanciale (cured pork jowl) is traditional, but pancetta or even thick-cut bacon can be substituted if guanciale is unavailable.

4

Cook the pasta according to package instructions until al dente. Reserve about 1 cup of the pasta cooking water before draining.

5

Working quickly, add the hot drained pasta to the skillet with the pancetta and toss to coat in the rendered fat. If the skillet has cooled too much, briefly heat it over low heat.

6

Remove the skillet from heat and quickly add the pasta to the bowl with the egg and cheese mixture. Toss rapidly to coat the pasta, adding a splash of the reserved pasta water as needed to create a creamy sauce. The residual heat from the pasta will cook the eggs without scrambling them.

7

Serve immediately, garnished with additional grated cheese, freshly ground black pepper, and chopped parsley if desired.

Recipe Notes

  • The key to creamy carbonara is working quickly once the pasta is cooked. Have all ingredients ready before cooking the pasta.
  • Never add cream to authentic carbonara. The creaminess comes from the emulsion of eggs, cheese, and pasta water.
  • If you're concerned about consuming raw eggs, use pasteurized eggs.
  • Carbonara will thicken as it cools, so serve immediately for the best texture.

Classic Lasagna

A hearty Italian dish with layers of pasta, rich meat sauce, creamy béchamel, and melted cheese. Perfect for family dinners and special occasions.

PREP TIME

30 mins

COOK TIME

1 hr 15 mins

SERVINGS

6 servings

CALORIES

700 kcal

Ingredients

Adjust servings:

6
12 lasagna noodles
1 lb ground beef
2 cups ricotta cheese
3 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
2 cups marinara sauce
1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
Salt and pepper, to taste
Fresh basil leaves, chopped (optional)

Instructions

1

Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).

2

Cook lasagna noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.

3

In a large skillet, brown the ground beef over medium heat. Drain excess fat.

4

Spread a thin layer of marinara sauce in the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish.

5

Layer noodles, ricotta, beef, mozzarella, and marinara sauce. Repeat layers.

6

Top with Parmesan cheese and bake for 45 minutes until bubbly and golden.

7

Let cool for 10 minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh basil if desired.

Recipe Notes

  • Use whole milk ricotta for creamier texture.
  • Substitute ground turkey for a leaner option.
  • Letting the lasagna rest helps it set for easier slicing.

Chicken Alfredo

A creamy pasta dish featuring grilled chicken, fettuccine, and a rich parmesan sauce. Perfect for a comforting meal.

PREP TIME

20 mins

COOK TIME

25 mins

SERVINGS

4 servings

CALORIES

600 kcal

Ingredients

Adjust servings:

4
12 oz fettuccine pasta
2 grilled chicken breasts, sliced
1 cup heavy cream
1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
2 tbsp butter
Salt and pepper, to taste
Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Instructions

1

Cook fettuccine pasta according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.

2

In a large skillet, melt butter over medium heat. Add heavy cream and bring to a simmer.

3

Stir in grated Parmesan cheese until sauce thickens.

4

Add sliced grilled chicken to the sauce and heat through.

5

Serve pasta topped with chicken and garnish with fresh parsley if desired.

Recipe Notes

  • Use freshly grated Parmesan for best flavor.
  • Substitute grilled shrimp for chicken for a seafood twist.
  • Adjust cream thickness by adding more or less heavy cream.
